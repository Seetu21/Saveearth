What is Deforestation?

Deforestation is the removal of forests and conversion of the land to non-forest use. This can be done for a variety of reasons, such as:

  • Agriculture
  • Grazing
  • Logging
  • Mining
  • Urbanization

Deforestation is a major environmental problem because forests play a vital role in the global ecosystem. Forests help to regulate the climate, protect biodiversity, and provide essential resources for people and wildlife.

Deforestation has a number of negative consequences, including:

  • Climate change
  • Loss of biodiversity
  • Soil erosion
  • Water cycle disruption
  • Increased risk of natural disasters
  • Negative impact on livelihoods
