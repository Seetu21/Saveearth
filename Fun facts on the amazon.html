Amazing Amazon Rainforest Facts

  1. The Amazon rainforest is the largest rainforest on Earth, covering an area of over 5.5 million square kilometers.


  2. The Amazon rainforest is home to over 10% of all known species on Earth.


  3. The Amazon rainforest produces more than 20% of the world's oxygen.


  4. The Amazon rainforest is a major source of freshwater for the world, with its rivers accounting for about 20% of the global runoff.


  5. The Amazon rainforest is a major carbon sink, absorbing about 10% of the world's carbon dioxide emissions.


  6. The Amazon rainforest is home to a variety of indigenous peoples, who have lived in the rainforest for thousands of years.


  7. The Amazon rainforest is home to the world's largest river by volume, the Amazon River.


  8. The Amazon rainforest is home to an estimated 3 million species of insects, including the world's largest insect, the Goliath beetle.


  9. The Amazon rainforest is home to an estimated 10 million plant species, including the world's largest tree, the giant sequoia.


  10. The Amazon rainforest is home to an estimated 3,000 species of fish, including the piranha and the arapaima.
